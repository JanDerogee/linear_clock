The linear clock is (from a mechanical point of view) very easy to build. The concept of a pointer running down a straight line gives in instant indication of the time without even reading the number next to the dial. The left side of the scale is night and morning, the right side of the scale is afternoon and evening. The simplicity of this clock makes it ideal for children that can read clocks. But also for authistic people (of which some have a distorded concept of time) this clock could be of use.
